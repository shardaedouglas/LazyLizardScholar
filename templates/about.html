About CyberStudy

Born from a passion for empowering the next generation through code, we're on a mission to make computer science education accessible, engaging, and transformative for every child.

Mission-Driven Child-Focused Future-Ready

Our Story

Every great mission begins with a single moment of inspiration. Here's how CyberStudy came to be.

The Spark of Inspiration

My journey into technology began as a childhood fascination, nurtured by a love for knowing how things worked and a mother who taught me that through knowledge, we have the power to shape our reality. I firmly believe that technology can change the entire trajectory of a child's life—just as it did for mine.

The moment of clarity came during my time as a Code Coach at theCoderSchool, working with a young girl who was initially shy and struggling with errors in her code. After we patiently worked together, she successfully fixed the bug herself. The immense pride and genuine excitement on her face as she saw her game character finally move correctly was unforgettable.

From Code Coach to Founder

As a Senior Software Engineer with over seven years of professional experience, I've built innovative software solutions, designed AI-powered applications, and developed games. But my most cherished experiences come from teaching game development and programming to students aged 5 to 18.

I don't just teach code; I mentor students through their projects, provide debugging support, and offer encouragement to help them deepen their understanding and achieve their goals. My approach is built on creating personalized learning paths and customized curriculums tailored to each student's individual learning style and goals.

The Vision

Our goal is to do more than just teach programming skills to little ones. We aspire to provide technological exposure to children from lower economical backgrounds empowering those children to see themselves as future leaders and innovators. We believe that greater diversity of perspectives breeds a medley of innovative ideas.

Our Mission & Values

We believe that computer science education should be accessible, engaging, and empowering for every child, regardless of their background or prior experience.

Inclusive Learning

Every child has the potential to excel in computer science. We create learning environments that celebrate diversity and ensure all students feel valued and supported.

Hands-On Approach

We believe in learning by doing. Our curriculum emphasizes practical, project-based learning that allows students to see immediate results from their efforts.

Growth Mindset

Mistakes are learning opportunities. We foster an environment where students feel safe to experiment, fail, and grow from their experiences.

Real-World Impact

We connect coding concepts to real-world applications, helping students understand how their skills can make a positive difference in their communities.

Digital Citizenship

Lifelong Learning

We instill a love of learning that extends beyond our classes, preparing students to adapt and thrive in an ever-changing technological landscape.

Our Teaching Approach

Young Learners (5-12)

We introduce foundational concepts like variables, loops, and conditional statements using engaging, low-code languages like Scratch, Microsoft MakeCode, and EduBlocks.

Advanced Students (13-18)

We dive into industry-standard languages such as Python, JavaScript, and C#/Unity, focusing on exciting projects in game development.

Why We Teach Kids to Code

Coding isn't just about technology—it's about developing essential life skills that will serve children throughout their entire lives.

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Coding teaches children to break down complex problems into manageable pieces, think logically, and develop systematic approaches to challenges. These skills are valuable in every aspect of life, not just technology.

Creativity & Self-Expression

Programming is a creative medium that allows children to express their ideas, build their own games, create art, and tell stories. It's like having a digital canvas where imagination becomes reality.

Collaboration & Communication

Modern programming is rarely done alone. Students learn to work in teams, share ideas, give and receive feedback, and communicate complex concepts clearly—skills essential for success in any career.

Future-Ready Skills

Technology is reshaping every industry. By learning to code, children develop the computational thinking and technical literacy needed to thrive in tomorrow's job market, regardless of their chosen career path.

Confidence & Persistence

There's nothing quite like the satisfaction of debugging a program and seeing it work perfectly. Coding builds confidence through small wins and teaches children that persistence and patience lead to success.

Global Impact

Today's children will solve tomorrow's challenges. By teaching them to code, we're empowering them to create solutions for climate change, healthcare, education, and other global issues that matter to them.

Our Teaching Philosophy

We believe that the best learning happens when children are engaged, challenged, and supported in equal measure.

Child-Centered Learning

Every child learns differently, and we honor that diversity. Our curriculum adapts to individual learning styles, interests, and pace, ensuring that each student feels successful and motivated to continue learning.

Play-Based Education

Learning should be fun! We use games, puzzles, and creative projects to make complex concepts accessible and engaging. When children are having fun, they're more likely to retain information and develop a genuine love for learning.

Hands-On Experience

We emphasize learning by doing. Students don't just learn about coding—they write code, debug programs, and create projects from day one. This practical approach builds confidence and deep understanding.

Celebrating Progress

Every step forward is worth celebrating. We recognize effort, persistence, and growth, not just perfect results. This positive reinforcement builds self-esteem and encourages students to take on new challenges.
